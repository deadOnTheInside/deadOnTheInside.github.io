Q: Why do you listen to what you do?

A: The simple answer? Because I love everything about it.

The long answer? I love all of the talent required to make it, the effort everyone in the bands put in.

I love all the instrumental parts, how they require skill to play, unlike most popular music today, and just the amazing sound people can get out of a guitar or three.

The solos and/or breakdowns generally involved in the music I like make it more complicated, with more than just verses, choruses, and a bridge.

Also, the lyrical content.

Most of the songs I listen to tell a story, often a very personal one to the band member(s) who wrote it.

I love the meanings behind every word they sing (or scream).

As well, the singers and screamers require talent as I refuse to listen to any talentless person who uses auto-tune.

Q: How can you stand all the screaming?

A: At first, I hated it. I thought the same thing.

But the more I listened and the more lyrics I read so I could understand what they were saying the more I liked it.

It’s just a different kind of singing. Like putting distortion on a guitar.

Except for the fact that it requires a ton of talent.

I love the screaming because you can’t auto-tune a scream, and they just sound so much better when left unedited.

The voice cracks, popping vocal chords, those little imperfections that let you know that there is indeed a very talented human on the other end.
